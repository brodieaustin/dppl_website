Story. Everyone has one. We contribute to our own every day. When we read, we are affected by other people's stories. At the Des Plaines Public Library, our book groups are a forum for readers to share how books reflect and shape their own stories.

One group meets every month on the first Tuesday at 10.00 am. Another one meets the second Thursday of the month at 7.30pm. Book groups meet in the Rotary Heritage Room on the 3rd floor. Copies of the book are provided a month in advance at the Readers Services desk.
 

September 2011 book discussion titles:

Tuesday Morning Group

The Interpreter of Maladies by Jhumpa Lahiri (1999), Tuesday, November 1 at 10 a.m. Register online. Discussion led by Head of Youth Services Stephanie Spetter.

Thursday Evening Group

The Paris Wife by Paula McLain(2011), Thursday, November 10 at 7:30 p.m. Register online. Discussion led by Readers Services Assistant Claire Green.

Books are always available one month prior at the Readers' Services Desk, 3rd floor.
