Proctoring Your Exam at the Library
We can serve as a proctoring site for your professional or student examination. There is no charge for this service. Please look at the following FAQs or contact Bob Blanchard at 847-376-2793 or bblanchard@dppl.org for more information. You may fax information to Bob Blanchard at 847-827-7974.

Any of the 4th floor Reference staff can act as your proctor when you take the test.

FAQs

When can I take my exam?
The library is open M-F 9 to 9, Saturday 9 to 5 and Sunday 1 to 5. We suggest you don't schedule your exam in the last hour the library is open.

My test needs to be taken online. Can you help me?
Yes. The computers for adults (over 15) are on the fourth floor. You can stop in at any time and we will get you on a computer. We can only sign you up for an hour: if you need more time, ask the Reference staff.

I have a paper test. How does that work?
We can book a private study room on the fourth floor for you. Call or email Bob Blanchard to do so (see above). The library staff cannot stay in the room with you, but they will check on you every so often during the exam period.

How do I get a copy of the finished paper exam back to my school?
We can fax a copy to the school. If you want us to mail the test, you or the school must provide an envelope and postage.
