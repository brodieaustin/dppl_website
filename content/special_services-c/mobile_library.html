Mobile Library

As of December 2011, the mobile library is longer in service.

But all the services you've grown to love from the mobile library – and even more - continue to be available at the main library located at 1501 Ellinwood Street across from the train station in downtown Des Plaines.

At the main library, you can:

  • Choose from more than 26,000 DVDs and 17,000 CDs, including more new releases and popular titles.
  • Read more than 400 daily, weekly and monthly newspapers and magazines.
  • Attend one of our free programs. With more than 75 programs a month, covering everything from baby book times to computer classes, musical performances to career networking groups, book groups to virtual bowling leagues, there's something for everyone.
  • Solve a Problem! Representatives of State Senator Dan Kotowski, Congresswoman Jan Schakowsky and State Representative Rosemary Mulligan have regular office hours at DPPL.
  • Check your email or surf the web from one of our 62 public access computers or access free Wi-Fi with your own laptop or mobile device.
  • Select from an even larger collection of popular fiction, non-fiction, mystery, romance, audiobooks, playaways and more.
  • Need assistance or a friendly recommendation? DPPL staff are ready and willing to help you find a book, or answer a reference question. Stop by the service desks on any floor for assistance.
  • Try out an eReader! You can now check one out for a four week loan period at DPPL! On the 3rd floor, you can also learn about using MyMediaMall to download free ebooks and audiobooks.
  • Explore all that the 2nd floor youth services department has to offer. Families with kids birth through 8th grades will find kid-content friendly computers with educational games, a huge selection of children's DVDs and CDs and books galore!
  • Get to know us – the staff is the heart of your public library.
Getting to DPPL is easy via Public Transportation

Metra's Union Pacific Northwest line – the Des Plaines stop is directly in front of (north of) the library. Six Pace bus lines also stop at the metra station or across miner Street from the station. Those Pace lines are: 208, 209, 226, 230, 234, 250.

For more information visit the Directions & Parking information on the website.
