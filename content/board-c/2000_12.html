Board Capsule December 19, 2000



The regular meeting of the Des Plaines Public Library Board of Trustees was held in the second floor conference on Tuesday, December 19, 2000. President Eldon Burk called the meeting to order at 7:30 PM.

PUBLIC COMMENTS AND QUESTIONS

Mr. Joseph Botte stated that he felt that there might have been some confusion by the public concerning the library budget, but that he felt the library money was well spent. Mr. Botte asked if the library was close to approaching their taxing limit and Sandra Norlin reported that the library does not have a taxing limit because of the Home Rule powers of the City of Des Plaines.

BOARD REVIEW OF DEPARTMENT ACTIVITIES.

Library Administrator Sandra Norlin reported on six new grants awarded to the Des Plaines Public Library in 2000. Inara Brubaker asked how the Des Plaines Public Library compares with other libraries in obtaining grant money. Sandra Norlin commented that it is necessary to study every grant before applying to ensure that it will benefit the library.

FINANCE COMMITTEE – Susan Burrows, Chairman.

Rhys Read questioned why Funds 415 and 202 were not included in the monthly Finance Report. The Board authorized Sandra Norlin to talk to Jim Egeberg about reporting Funds 415 and 202 in the monthly Finance Report. Eldon Burk asked the Finance Committee to request a meeting with the Director of Finance, Jim Egeberg.

MANAGEMENT COMMITTEE – Ellen Yearwood, Chairman.

Ellen Yearwood reported that the Management Committee met on Tuesday, December 12, 2000. The Committee discussed revisions to the Meeting Room Use Policy and the Bylaws of the Library Board of Trustees.

MOTION by Committee to approve revisions to the Meeting Room Use Policy and the Bylaws of the Library Board of Trustees. Vote: Ayes: All. Nays: None. MOTION CARRIED.

Leslie Steiner will post the new Meeting Room Use Policy on the library Internet site.

PLANNING COMMITTEE - John Burke.

John Burke reported on the progress made by the Planning Committee on the Strategic Plan. The Board will review and approve the Strategic Plan when the Committee has completed it. John Burke asked the Board for their suggestions and comments.

John Burke and Sandra Norlin met with Walter Roth and his associates from Executive Service Corps to discuss new goals and objectives for the Board, a five-year plan and review a first draft of a questionnaire for the Board. The Board retreat will take place on Saturday, April 21, 2001, with a location to be determined at a later date.

FRIENDS OF THE LIBRARY – Inara Brubaker.

Inara Brubaker reported that the Friends met on Tuesday, November 28, 2000 for their regular meeting. Mary Ann Brown, Head of Youth Services and Margie Borris attended the meeting to promote volunteerism in the library.

The shelf program is being discussed as an option for the new library, with a location to be determined at a later date.

ADMINISTRATOR'S REPORT

Mary Ann Brown, Head of Youth Services has resigned for personal reasons, effective December 30, 2000. She has accepted a position at Algonquin Public Library District, which is her home library.

Joseph Jacobs, Library Page resigned effective November 25, 2000.

Florin Felecan will begin work as a full-time Maintenance Assistant on December 18, 2000. Deborah Samborski began as Security Monitor on December 13, 2000.

We held a quarterly All-Staff Meeting on December 14. Staff photos were taken, we heard a report from the Healthy Community Partnerships Committee, and experienced some magic.

The staff holiday party was held December 15.

We have received word that NSLS received a grant to continue our participation in the Learning Organization activities. This segment will focus on work and job redesign.

We have held two orientation sessions this month.

All staff members contributed to the success of the first city holiday lighting ceremonies held in Library Plaza. Gary Valente, Chris Posinger, and Lee Cho and the Youth Services staff deserve special recognition for their cooperation in planning and providing special events.

Sandra Norlin participated in the Illinois Library Leadership Retreat in Urbana on November 30 and December 1. Sandra Norlin has taken two basic classes on Excel, arranged by the City of Des Plaines and attended the City Holiday Party on December 7.

Mr. Botte commented to the Board that he has noticed many more new parents with children at the new building and that the children seem to be in a learning mode.

NEW BUSINESS

Susan Burrows reported that the 2001 Salary Schedule includes a 3.5% general wage increase for personnel.

MOTION by Susan Burrows, seconded by Ellen Yearwood, to approve the 2001 Salary Schedule, which includes a 3.5% general wage increase for personnel to become effective January 1, 2001. ROLL CALL VOTE: AYES: Brubaker, Burk, Burke, Burrows, Ciborowski, Grice, Read, Yearwood. NAYS: None. MOTION CARRIED.

MOTION by Susan Burrows, seconded by John Burke, to approve the transfer of funds within the 2000 Library Budget to cover all 2000 appropriation deficit categories and to transfer any remaining balance to the Library Fund for 2001. ROLL CALL VOTE: AYES: Brubaker, Burk, Burke, Burrows, Ciborowski, Grice, Read, Yearwood. NAYS: None. MOTION CARRIED.

The Finance Committee will meet to discuss transfer of funds to the Library Capital Project Fund rather than the Library Fund.

MOTION by Susan Burrows, seconded by Inara Brubaker, to approve the 2001 appropriation and the 2000 levy and to direct Carol Kidd to deliver the information to the City of Des Plaines. ROLL CALL VOTE: AYES: Brubaker, Burk, Burke, Burrows, Ciborowski, Grice, Read, Yearwood. NAYS: None. MOTION CARRIED.

The Board discussed potential Board membership on the Arts Council. Susan Burrows and John Ciborowski will contact the Arts Council for additional information on meeting dates.

Library Administrator, Sandra Norlin, reported that she has been asked to become a candidate for the office of president for ILA, which is a three-year commitment. Sandra Norlin asked the Board for their support and reported that ILA meets 4 –6 times a year.

MOTION by William Grice, seconded by Inara Brubaker, to support Sandra Norlin's candidacy for the president of ILA. Vote: Ayes: All. Nays: None. MOTION CARRIED.

MOTION by Ellen Yearwood, seconded by Inara Brubaker, to approve the corrections to the Illinois Public Library Annual Report for 1999-2000. Vote: Ayes: All. Nays: None. MOTION CARRIED.

Eldon Burk distributed information on the design of a plaque for the lobby of the building and the Board discussed placement of the names. The Board will vote on the plaque when a final decision has been reached.
