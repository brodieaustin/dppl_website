Patron Services

We are nearing our goal of one million items circulated during 2003. With total circulation to date at 907,121, and November and December estimates of 87,000 and 75,000, the millionth circulation should occur during the first or second week of December. A staff committee is planning a celebration for the public on the day the millionth item is circulated.

During the month of October we increased our circulation by 13.26% over the previous October. Our year to date circulation has increased 11.31% over this period in 2002. Children's items accounted for 38% of the total items circulated. The Mobile Library circulation increased by 17% over the previous October. Audiovisual items accounted for 43% of the total items circulated (Children's, 31% and Adult's 51%). Patrons used Self-Check to check out 23% of the items circulated.

The North Suburban Library System's annual report listed Des Plaines Reciprocal Borrowing activities as 40,271 items borrowed from other libraries by Des Plaines cardholders and 64,958 items lent from our collection to cardholders of other libraries.

The November 2003 issue of American Libraries includes the article "Public Library Circulation and Expenditures Increase in 2002", [p. 54-55]. The report indicates that taken as a whole, U.S. public libraries ' circulation and expenditures both increased in 2002. The median circulation per capita throughout the U.S. for 2002 was 6.1; Des Plaines Public Library was 16.9. The median expenditure per capita was $23.82; Des Plaines' was $79. The median for expenditures per item circulated was $4.06; Des Plaines' was $4.70. The median for material expenditures per capita was $3.43; Des Plaines' was $12.63. The median percent of the budget for materials was 15%; Des Plaines' was 16%.

The contract will begin December 1, 2003 and end June 26, 2005.

MOTION by William Grice, seconded by Susan Burrows, to enter into an agreement with Majesty Maintenance for janitorial services for the Des Plaines Public Library and Mobile Library in the amount of $3,385.00 per month to begin December 1, 2003 and end June 26, 2005 and to terminate the current janitorial agreement with Crystal Maintenance Service Corp. effective November 30, 2003. Sandra Norlin asked for board approval to purchase 16 Dell computers and monitors in the amount of $15,344. Sandra Norlin explained that the purchase was approved at the August 19, 2003 board meeting, but that the line item for computer hardware has exceeded the budgeted amount. Sandra explained that this was due to computer hardware that had been purchased with grant monies and that problems with 3M equipment in 2002 resulted in invoices budgeted for in 2002 not being paid until 2003.

MOTION by Rhys Read, seconded by Susan Burrows, to approve the purchase of 16 Dell computers and monitors at a cost of $15,344, which is in the best interest of the library and that the contingency reserve fund my be used to purchase these computers if needed. ROLL CALL VOTE: AYES: Burrows, Grice, Lake, Mahony, Read, Tejcek, Weinberg. NAYS: None. MOTION CARRIED.
