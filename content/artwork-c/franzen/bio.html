Joan Grommes Franzen




Biography

Joan Grommes Franzen studied at the University of Wisconsin, Madison, and the American Academy of Art, Chicago, Illinois, receiving degrees in both Fine Art and Commercial Art. After working in the commercial field for 5 years, she moved on to specialize in pastel and watercolor in 1969.

Her works are currently displayed at J'Marc Galleries, Lake Geneva, WI; Deer Path Gallery, Lake Forest, IL; and Art Expo, St. Pete Beach, FL. Franzen was a Merit Award Winner in the "Wisconsin Watercolor 2000 Competition" and was accepted in the "Wisconsin Watercolor 2001 Competition.” She has been in national and regional shows where she has won numerous "Best of Show,” "First Place," and "Purchase” awards. Her work is in many corporate and private collections in the United States and abroad. She does many special assignment commissions: landscapes, seascapes, still life, boats, and portraits of all kinds. 

Franzen has been a member of the Palette and Chisel Academy of Fine Art, Chicago, IL, since 1967; a member of the National Society of Arts and Letters, Evanston, IL Chapter; associate member of the Midwest Watercolor Society; associate member of the American Watercolor Society; and a member of numerous art league organizations in various states.

Franzen received a grant for instructing students in pastel at Maine East High School, Park Ridge, IL. She says, "This was one of the most rewarding and gratifying experiences anyone could have." She conducts annual workshops in Wisconsin and teaches pastel and watercolor classes in Illinois and Wisconsin. She gives demonstrations, lectures, and private instruction.
