Tina Mazzoni Solarz




Artist's statement

My love of art started at a very young age with drawing things around me, especially people. I drew sketches of my teachers and fellow students as early as 3rd grade and was encouraged to attend group classes at the Art Institute of Chicago by being given a scholarship in 7th grade.

My subject matter ranges from landscapes to people enjoying flowers or a sunset -- whatever I see that inspires me to try to capture a moment of light and shadow or the remembrance of things past. I have been described as a romantic impressionist. I see myself as wanting to record beauty - perhaps the light dancing across a flower or flesh, but always to make people see the beauty in life.

My paintings are done mostly on sandpaper, with some done on pastel paper, board or Wallis paper. Pastels are pigment in a stick form, the same pigment used to make watercolor or oil. They are extremely permanent when protected by glass. The technique I use is mainly the stroking of pastel in numerous layers so that many colors show through, creating a myriad of hues.

My ideas come from watching nature and by setting up scenes for figures, complete with clothing and props, and then photographing them. From the hundreds of prints I have, I choose several and create a unique design, which I then use as the starting point of my painting. I feel that to create a thing of beauty is indeed a joy forever.
