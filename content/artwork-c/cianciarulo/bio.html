Bette Cianciarulo


Biography

Bette Cianciarulo has drawn and “doodled” all her life. She attended Forest Elementary and Algonquin Schools, and graduated from Maine West High School in 1969. Subsequently, she attended Harper Jr. College and Oakton Junior College.

Cianciarulo has been married since 1973 and became a professional artist in 1987. She now works in acrylics and does mostly landscapes. She has been working as a teaching assistant since 1986 at Cumberland School, and has paintings hanging in the teachers' lounge at the school.

She has shown her work at the Blue Moon Gallery in Skokie, the Prairie Lee Gallery in Des Plaines, and in Omaha, Nebraska.

Artist's Statement:

I paint early in the morning, everyday, at Cumberland School before school starts. My image of what is in my mind is in my paintings. I like when people look at my work and can put themselves "there" or say, "I have been there". Then I feel I have accomplished what I set out to do in my painting.

6/4/02
