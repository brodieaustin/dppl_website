Peg Kartinos


Biography

Education: B.A. Chemistry, Vanderbilt University, 1944
M.A. Chemistry, Indiana University, 1946
Career: Helped set up Chemistry lab at Lutheran General Hospital.
Taught chemistry to freshman nursing class at Lutheran General Hospital.
1980 - Switched from left-brain to right brain, enrolled at Oakton Community College to study drawing with Jim Krauss and still attending his classes; Independent Study Critique.

Peg has developed an unique process for creating bird portraits (the results are so realistic that she has been accused many times of killing birds for their feathers) and semi-abstract feather designs; all of which reflect her love for birds, their colors and patterns. Her work was featured in The Artist Magazine, Jan. 1990. She has appeared on television programs:  Artistically Speaking and Interesting Artist and Their Unusual Work. Pioneer Press did a cover article on her.

Peg has had numerous commissions and her work is found in many public and private collections. Her work has won many awards. Her demonstrations in schools, art supply stores and art league meetings are always well received. Many galleries have featured Peg's work in one person and collective shows. At present she is represented by Copper Frog Galler in Long Grove, IL.

Artist's statement

My reverence for nature dominates my life, and painting is a way of expressing that feeling. I paint because I have compulsion to create. When working in my studio, I lose myself and feel in touch with the universal creative process.

6/19/02
