Ruth Anne Monaghan


Biography

Ruth Ann Monaghan was born in the northern farmland of Iowa. Country landscape has always been her favorite subject. Being an Audubon Society member has been reflected in her artwork of birds.

Ruth has studied fine art at Northwestern and The Art Institute of Chicago and with some of the top midwest artists. She has shown her work and won numerous awards in the Chicagoland area. Her work is represented in the Sears Collection, YMCA Regional Offices, 1st National Bank of Chicago, National Bank of LaGrange and many private homes.

She gathers much of her inspiration while at her home in a valley near La Crosse, Wisconsin.

Artist's statement

"My collages are a series of paper and paint used over and under each other. I like a piece of work that doesn't bore the viewer after the second look. Sometimes I use real flowers or weeds found in the woods, or glitter, metallic paint or whatever as an additional surprise. Some of my pieces are called "beautiful" or "pretty" and tend to make people smile and feel good. For those comments and feelings, I offer no apology, but take delight.
