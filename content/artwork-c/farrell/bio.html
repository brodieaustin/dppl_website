Barbara Farrell


Biography

Barbara Farrell is an accomplished, award-winning watercolorist. Farrell is basically self-taught, learning her craft through experimentation and by reading. She has also taken courses from professionals such as Nancy Fortunato, Phil Austin, and Lou Taylor. She has studied figure drawing at the American Academy of Art with Bill Parks.

Farrell is past president of the Glenview Art League and a member of many of the art leagues in the Chicago area, as well as the Washington Island Art Association. She has also demonstrated throughout the Chicago Area. She was an artist member of the Palette and Chisel Academy in Chicago before moving to Wisconsin, and is Vice President of the Illinois Watercolor Society.

Artist's statement

The paints and paper I use are the highest quality. All the paints are rated permanent and lightfast. My paintings are not produced by a formula process. Sometimes they are planned in an organized manner: a sketch, first wash, second wash, and lastly, detail. There are occasions, when color is applied spontaneously and the painting emerges. Both processes have helped my growth as an artist.

My wildlife, still-life, and landscape paintings are filled with the rich color which has become my trademark. The subjects I choose are familiar objects, places, and people. All of my paintings are original concepts. My still-lifes are either painted from life, or I use photographs that I have shot for reference material. Usually I paint a series of paintings, i.e., American Flag, Patio scenes, Vegetables, Fruits, Landscapes.
