Maria Rawski


Biography

Maria Rawski was born in Barcelona, Spain, and has lived in England and Switzerland. She has traveled throughout Europe, visiting museums and galleries. She has also lived in New York for one year while working with the Spanish delegation to the United Nations. During that time, she attended lectures on painting at the Metropolitan Museum of Art.

Rawski became interested in Chinese brush painting when she saw a demonstration at the Art Institute of Chicago. She has studied with several Chinese teachers during the last 10 years. She uses a wide range of colors, and her work is done with Chinese watercolors made with natural roots and minerals. The silk, the colors, and the brushes come from Hong Kong.

Rawski paints mainly landscapes, birds, flowers, and butterflies. She paints both large scale and smaller works. She has been inspired by the old masters of the Ming, Sung, and Tang dynasties.

Her works are featured in collections in the United States, Canada, England, Germany, Poland, Spain, and Switzerland, and she has won many awards.
