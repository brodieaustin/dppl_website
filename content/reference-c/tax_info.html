Local Programs

Free preparation of 2011 income tax returns include:

CEDA Northwest

CEDA Northwest, 1300 W. Northwest Highway in Mt. Prospect, is offering free income tax preparation of 2011 returns to residents of Cook County (regardless of age) with a gross annual income of less than $50,000. Preparation is on a first-come, first-served basis (no appointments), on Saturdays, starting February 4, through April 14, from 9 a.m. until 12noon. Bring the following information:

  • Photo ID (driver's license or state ID)
  • Social security cards for yourself and everyone in the household
  • Last year's federal and state income tax returns
  • Current tax forms as well as W-2s and 1099s (1099s are for the self-employed)
  • Information about any other income, deductions or credits, such as high medical bills, or the amount of money received from stocks and bonds
  • The name, address and identification number of a qualified caregiver if you have caregiver expenses.
  • A checkbook, as transmission by direct deposit is considered faster and safer than postal mail.

If you have questions, call 847-392-2332.

LIFT-Evanston

Low-income persons and families can contact LIFT-Evanston for free income tax preparation by trained student volunteers from Northwestern University. Tax preparation is available from:

  • January 30 through April 13.
  • Hours: 9 a.m.–5 p.m., Monday through Friday (open Tuesdays until 7 p.m.)
  • Saturdays: February 11 and 25, March 31 and April 7, from 10 a.m.–3 p.m.

LIFT-Evanston cannot file your return if:

  • You earned over: $50,000 (gross annual income) with qualifying children
  • You earned over: $25,000 (gross annual income) if single.
  • You or your spouse itemized deductions on last year’s tax return.
  • You or your spouse mortgaged or rented-out property (such as house or land) in 2011.
  • You or your spouse owned stocks, mutual funds or cashed a CD in 2011. You or your spouse was a member of the armed forces in 2011.

All tax clients must bring:

  • A valid picture ID of yourself
  • all W-2s, 1099 and income/expense-related forms
  • a copy of last year's tax return, if you have one
  • the Social Security Cards or the Tax Identification Numbers (ITIN) cards for everybody shown on your tax return (including dependents).

Additional forms may be needed:

  1. If you are married and filing a joint return with your spouse, both you and your spouse will need to be present during the appointment.
  2. If you have a checking account and want your refund to be deposited into it, please bring your check book.
  3. If you or your spouse received unemployment compensation in 2011, please bring a copy of your 1099-G form.
  4. If you or your spouse received any social security benefits in 2011, please bring a copy of your SSA-1099 form.
  5. If your or your spouse earned interest over $50 in a savings account or saving bond in 2011, please bring a copy of your 1099-INT form.
  6. If you or your spouse transferred or withdrew funds from an IRA in 2011, please bring a copy of your 1099-R.
  7. If you or your spouse paid or received alimony in 2011, please bring a record of the amount pay or received.
  8. If you or your spouse or a dependant paid tuition in 2011, please bring a copy of your 1098-T form.
  9. If you or your spouse paid interest on a student loan in 2011, please bring a copy of your 1098-E form.
  10. If you received an advance on your child tax credit this year, please bring a copy of IRS Notice 1319. If you do not have this form, please tell the tax preparer of the child credit advance during your appointment.
  11. If you or your spouse paid for child care (day care, summer day camp, after school programs or a baby sitter legally paid) you could receive a credit. Please bring the child care provider’s fill name, address and amount paid. Also bring the provider’s social security number (SSN) if they are an individual or employer identification number (EIN) if they are an organization. You will need to call the child care provider to obtain this information.

If any of the necessary forms have not been mailed to you, you must call the company or government agency to have a copy of the correct form(s) sent.

For more information and to schedule an appointment, call: 847-491-6707.
