Peace Pole




The peace pole reminds us to keep peace ever present in our thoughts and to strive for harmony rather than conflict. The inscription on the library peace pole reads, "May peace prevail on earth," in eight languages. 

The eight languages were chosen for a variety of reasons.

  • English - predominant language in Des Plaines and the country.

  • German - Des Plaines has a rich German heritage.

  • Sign language - to represent the disabled community.

  • Spanish, Polish, Russian, Filipino, and Gujarati - they are the dominant languages of children in local school districts who speak a language other than English at home.

Peace Pole Project 

The Peace Pole Project began in Japan, under the auspices of The World Peace Prayer Society, a non-profit, non-denominational organization founded in 1955 by Masahisa Goi. Friends and supporters of the project have dedicated over 200,000 peace poles in 180 countries around the world. Peace poles have been planted in such diverse sites as Gorky Park, the Pyramids of El Giza, and the Magnetic North Pole. People from all walks of life have dedicated Peace Poles. They include former President Jimmy Carter, former Pope John Paul II, the Dalai Lama, and folk singer John Denver.

Library Peace Pole Dedication

The peace pole was dedicated on the library plaza on September 30, 2001, during the first anniversary celebration for the current building. City and library officials spoke at the ceremony. Staff and patrons recited the peace pole inscription in many different languages. Father Gene Dyer of the Des Plaines Ministerial Society recited the following words:

Deep Peace (adapted from the traditional Irish/Gaelic)

Deep Peace of the running wave to you
Deep Peace of the flowing air to you
Deep Peace of the quiet earth to you
Deep Peace of the shining stars to you
Deep Peace of the gentle night to you
Moon and stars pour their healing light on/you
Deep Peace to you.

The library peace pole was funded by a Cultural Diversity Grant from the North Suburban Library System.
