Frequently Asked Questions About Wireless Internet Access

  1. Does the library offer a wireless connection to patrons with laptops?

    Yes. The library offers wireless network access (hotspots) throughout the building to patrons with wireless capable devices, such as wireless capable laptop and handheld computers. You must provide your own device configured for wireless access.
     

  2. What do I need to connect to a wireless hotspot in the library?

    You need a laptop or handheld computer that is configured to conform to standard 802.11b, commonly known as Wi-Fi. New laptops may come already configured for wireless.
     

  3. Is it difficult to connect to a wireless hotspot?

    Not usually. Most laptops that come configured for wireless access can automatically pick up the wireless signal. You may have to know how to use your wireless device's configuration software to connect to the wireless network.
     

  4. Is technical support available from the library staff?

    You can get general help from the 4th floor technology page. You must be familiar with your device and know how to set it up for wireless access.
     

  5. When can I use the wireless access?

    Wireless access is available to patrons during library business hours.
     

  6. Is there a charge for using the wireless access?

    There is no charge for using the library's wireless access with your wireless capable device.
     

  7. Can I connect to the printers and other software via the wireless network?

    Yes - see the staff on the 4th floor for assistance or read the instructions that follow.
