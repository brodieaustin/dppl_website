Filling out forms

Believe it or not, surfing the Net requires you to fill out a lot of forms!

Some will be short forms that are easy to fill out. Others will be complicated and will require a lot of patience to complete.

Sample Form Exercise

Fill out this sample form.

Start by typing inside the boxes. Click in the first box so that you see a blinking cursor. Move from box to box by clicking inside each box or just hit the Tab key.

This form also requires you to make multiple choice selections in three different ways.

  • The round circles are called radio buttons. Click inside the button to make your choice.
  • The box labeled "Select" is called a pull down menu or pull down box. Click on the box and a list appears. As you move your cursor up and down the list, the item underneath your cursor is highlighted. Click on your choice and and it moves up the list to appear in the box.
  • The next selection will be made by cliking in the check boxes. Unlike the radio buttons, the check boxes allow you to make more than one choice. You can also uncheck, or deselect your choices.

When you are done, click on the Submit form button.

Some tips to remember:

  • Most web sites will alert you if you make any errors or forget to fill out any part of the form.
  • Many of the forms you fill out will take you to a new page in the web site when you click Submit or press Enter. In our case your choices appear in the box at the bottom of the form.
  • Some forms have a Reset button that clears out everything you entered in the form.
